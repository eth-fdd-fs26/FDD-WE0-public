        Pump Failure Prediction— Data Cleaning & Fitting (Ankita):
        The primary coolant pump is a critical point of failure. If it goes down, the core overheats. The manager has access to historical logs from the pump, but the logs are notoriously messy—sensors drop offline, inputs get duplicated, and formats vary.
        The primary learning objective here is the data pipeline: handling missing values (NaNs), removing outliers, normalizing data, and finally fitting a robust scikit-learn model like a Random Forest or Logistic Regression to predict a "Failure" (1) or "Safe" (0) state within the next hour.
        Synthetic Dataset Generation:
        Dataset Size: 10,000 rows of data.
        Failure Ratio: It sets a 5% failure rate, meaning 9,500 rows will represent healthy pump operations, and 500 rows will represent pump failures.
        The script establishes four specific pump models (PMP-001 through PMP-004). Each pump has intrinsic properties:
        Inspection Rating: A score from 1 to 5.
        Base Runtime: How many hours the pump has already been operating (ranging from 1,000 to 13,000 hours).
        Failure Weight: The probability that a failure belongs to this specific pump. Older pumps with higher runtimes (like PMP-001) are heavily weighted to fail more often than newer pumps (like PMP-004).
         Generating Telemetry (The Core Logic): The generate_telemetry function acts as the physics engine of the script. It creates realistic-looking sensor readings using normal statistical distributions (np.random.normal).
        For Healthy Pumps: It generates standard baseline readings for flow rate, inlet/outlet pressure, vibration, bearing temperature, motor current, and motor voltage.
        For Failing Pumps: It simulates three distinct types of failures, altering the sensor data to match the specific physical symptoms of that failure mode:
        Mechanical Failure (50% of failures): Triggers a significant increase in vibration amplitude and bearing temperature.
        Hydraulic Failure (30% of failures): Causes a massive drop in flow rate and a corresponding drop in motor current (cavitation).
        Stall Failure (20% of failures): Results in a drop in outlet pressure accompanied by a massive spike in motor current.
